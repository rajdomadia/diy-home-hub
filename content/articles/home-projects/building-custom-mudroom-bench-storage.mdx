## Why Every Home Needs a Mudroom Bench

A mudroom bench is the unsung hero of home organization. It provides a dedicated spot for removing shoes, storing bags, and transitioning from the chaos of the outside world to the comfort of your home. When you build one with integrated storage, you multiply its usefulness exponentially.

A well-designed mudroom bench eliminates the pile of shoes by the door, gives each family member a designated spot for their belongings, and creates a clean, welcoming entry to your home. And when you build it yourself, you can customize every dimension to fit your specific space and needs.

## Design and Planning

### Measuring Your Space

Before cutting a single piece of wood, take detailed measurements of your mudroom or entryway. Measure the available wall length, the distance from the wall to any opposing wall or obstruction, and the ceiling height if you plan to add hooks or a shelf above the bench.

A comfortable bench seat height is between 17 and 19 inches. Standard bench depth is 15 to 18 inches, which provides comfortable seating without extending too far into the walkway. Length depends on your space, but plan for at least 18 inches of seating width per person.

### Storage Options

The most common storage configuration is an open-top bench with compartments underneath. This design provides easy access to shoes and bags while keeping them hidden from view. Consider these storage approaches based on your needs.

**Open cubbies** below the seat are the simplest to build and provide quick access. They work well for frequently used shoes and bags but collect dust and can look messy.

**Hinged top storage** turns the entire seat into a lid that opens to reveal a large storage cavity. This design is cleaner looking but requires standing up to access the contents.

**Pull-out drawers** offer the most polished look and best organization but are the most complex to build. They add significant cost in hardware and require precise construction.

For this guide, we will build a bench with open cubbies below and a hinged top for additional hidden storage — the best of both approaches.

## Materials List

**Lumber:**
- Two sheets of 3/4-inch birch plywood (for the body, seat, and dividers)
- Four pieces of 1x3 select pine (for face frames)
- Two pieces of 1x2 select pine (for edge banding)
- One piece of 1x4 pine (for the toe kick)

**Hardware:**
- Two continuous (piano) hinges, 36 inches long
- Eight L-brackets for internal reinforcement
- 1-1/4 inch pocket hole screws
- 1-5/8 inch wood screws
- Wood glue
- 120-grit and 220-grit sandpaper
- Paint or stain and polyurethane

**Tools:**
- Table saw or circular saw with a straightedge guide
- Miter saw for trim cuts
- Drill and driver
- Pocket hole jig
- Clamps (at least four bar clamps)
- Tape measure, square, and pencil
- Level
- Safety glasses and hearing protection

## Step-by-Step Construction

### Step 1: Cut the Plywood Panels

For a bench that is 48 inches long, 18 inches deep, and 18 inches tall, cut the following pieces from your plywood.

Cut two side panels at 18 inches tall by 18 inches deep. Cut the bottom panel at 48 inches long by 18 inches deep. Cut the back panel at 48 inches long by 18 inches tall. Cut two internal dividers at 17.25 inches tall by 17.25 inches deep (slightly shorter to account for the bottom panel thickness). Cut the seat panel at 48 inches long by 18 inches deep.

Mark each piece clearly with a pencil so you know which is which during assembly. A quick sketch of the bench with dimensions noted is invaluable during construction.

### Step 2: Drill Pocket Holes

Using your pocket hole jig set for 3/4-inch material, drill pocket holes along the bottom edges of the side panels and back panel, the tops of the dividers, and the back edge of the bottom panel.

Pocket holes create strong, hidden joints that are perfect for plywood construction. They are much faster than traditional joinery methods and produce joints that are more than strong enough for a bench.

### Step 3: Assemble the Box

Begin by attaching the bottom panel to the back panel using pocket hole screws and wood glue. The back panel should sit on top of the bottom panel, creating an L-shape.

Next, attach the two side panels. Apply glue to all mating surfaces and drive pocket hole screws from the inside. Use a square to verify that each corner is 90 degrees before the glue begins to set.

Install the internal dividers at even intervals. For a 48-inch bench with two dividers, space them at 16 inches from each end, creating three equal cubbies. Check that each divider is plumb using a level and secure with pocket screws from below.

Reinforce all joints with L-brackets on the inside corners. These add significant rigidity to the structure, especially important for a piece of furniture that will support the weight of seated adults.

### Step 4: Add the Face Frame

Cut your 1x3 pine pieces to create a face frame that covers the raw plywood edges at the front of the bench. The face frame consists of two vertical stiles the full height of the bench, a top rail, and a bottom rail.

Attach the stiles first using glue and finishing nails or pocket screws from behind. Then install the top and bottom rails between the stiles. The face frame should be flush with the outer edges of the plywood box.

### Step 5: Build the Hinged Seat

The seat panel gets a 1x2 edge band on the front edge to hide the plywood layers and provide a finished appearance. Glue and nail the 1x2 to the front edge of the seat panel.

Attach the continuous hinges along the back edge of the seat, aligning them with the back of the bench. The seat should open upward like a chest lid, providing access to the storage area on top of the cubbies.

Consider adding a slow-close lid support on each side to prevent the seat from slamming shut on small fingers. These inexpensive mechanisms are well worth the additional safety and convenience.

### Step 6: Install the Toe Kick

A toe kick is a recessed panel at the bottom front of the bench that provides clearance for your feet when sitting. Cut the 1x4 to length and install it recessed about two inches back from the face frame. Attach it with screws from inside the bench.

### Step 7: Sand and Finish

Sand the entire bench starting with 120-grit paper to smooth all surfaces and round over any sharp edges. Follow with 220-grit for a smooth finish. Pay special attention to the seat surface and the face frame, which are the most visible and touched areas.

For a painted finish, apply a coat of primer followed by two coats of semi-gloss latex paint. Sand lightly with 320-grit between coats for the smoothest result.

For a natural wood finish, apply wood conditioner to the pine face frame pieces first, then stain to your desired color. Finish with three coats of polyurethane, sanding with 320-grit between coats.

### Step 8: Secure to the Wall

Attach the bench to the wall by driving screws through the back panel into wall studs. Use at least four screws, two on each end, hitting studs for maximum security. This prevents the bench from tipping when someone sits on the edge or when the lid is open.

## Customization Ideas

**Add hooks above the bench** for coats, backpacks, and umbrellas. Mount a board to the wall above the bench and install hooks at staggered heights for family members of different sizes.

**Install shelf above the hooks** for baskets, hats, or seasonal items. A shelf 12 inches deep mounted 48 inches above the bench seat works well.

**Label each cubby** with family members' names using small chalkboard labels or painted wooden tags. This encourages everyone to keep their belongings in their assigned space.

**Add cushions** to the seat for comfort. A custom cushion with a washable cover makes the bench inviting and adds color to your entryway.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What type of plywood is best for this project?**
Birch plywood offers the best combination of smooth surface, strength, and affordability. If you plan to paint, you can use lower-grade plywood and fill any voids with wood filler.

**Can I modify the dimensions for a smaller space?**
Absolutely. Adjust the length to fit your available wall space. You can also reduce the depth to 15 inches for tighter spaces, though the storage capacity will be reduced.

**How much weight will this bench support?**
With proper construction and wall mounting, this bench can comfortably support 300 to 400 pounds distributed across the seat. The pocket hole joints and plywood construction create a very strong structure.
